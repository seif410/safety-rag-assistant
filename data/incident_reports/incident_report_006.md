# Incident Report #006

**Date:** 2024-07-14
**Site:** 10th of Ramadan Industrial Zone, Site B
**Severity:** Critical
**Category:** Scaffolding — Structural Failure
**Shift:** Morning (06:00 - 14:00)
**Reported By:** Construction Foreman - Tarek Abdel-Fattah

## Description

At approximately 13:10, a section of tubular frame scaffolding collapsed at the east wall of Building 3 during exterior painting operations. Two workers were on the scaffold platform at the time of failure, at an approximate height of 9 meters. The scaffolding had been erected three days earlier by the site carpentry crew rather than by certified scaffolding erectors.

Post-collapse inspection revealed that base plates were placed directly on unprepared sandy soil without mudsills, and cross-bracing was missing from two bays on the lower section. One section of the scaffold had been modified by workers to add a cantilevered platform extension using unsecured planks.

## Injuries

- Worker 1: Fractured right wrist and multiple contusions from the fall. Transported to hospital.
- Worker 2: Bruised ribs and lacerations. Treated on-site and transported to hospital for imaging.
- Combined 42 lost workdays across both workers.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Scaffold erected by unqualified personnel without following manufacturer specifications.
2. **Contributing Factor:** Base plates placed on unprepared ground — no mudsills or solid foundation.
3. **Contributing Factor:** Missing cross-bracing in lower bays reduced structural integrity.
4. **Contributing Factor:** Unauthorized modification (cantilevered plank extension) added eccentric loading.
5. **Systemic Issue:** No scaffold inspection by a competent person before workers were allowed on the platform.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1926.451(c)(2) — Scaffolds must be erected on base plates and mudsills or other adequate firm foundation.
- 29 CFR 1926.451(c)(3) — Supported scaffold poles, legs, posts, and frames shall be braced.
- 29 CFR 1926.452(c)(6) — Frames and panels shall be braced by cross, horizontal, or diagonal braces.
- 29 CFR 1926.454(a) — Scaffolds shall be erected, moved, dismantled, or altered only under the supervision of a competent person.

## Corrective Actions

1. All scaffolding on-site dismantled and rebuilt by certified scaffold erectors (completed 2024-07-18).
2. Mandatory competent person inspection before any scaffold is released for use — inspection tag system implemented.
3. Only certified scaffold erectors authorized to erect, modify, or dismantle scaffolding.
4. Ground preparation requirements (mudsills, leveling) added to scaffold erection checklist.
5. Workers who modified the scaffold received formal disciplinary action and retraining.
6. Weekly scaffold integrity inspections added to safety program.

## Follow-Up

Worker 1 returned to modified duty 2024-08-12. Worker 2 returned to full duty 2024-07-28. Scaffolding competency training scheduled for all construction crews by 2024-08-15.
