# Incident Report #009

**Date:** 2024-09-02
**Site:** Al-Obour Industrial Complex, Site A
**Severity:** High
**Category:** Respiratory Protection
**Shift:** Morning (06:00 - 14:00)
**Reported By:** Industrial Hygienist - Dr. Sara Adel

## Description

During a routine industrial hygiene walkthrough at 10:00, the industrial hygienist observed four workers performing sandblasting operations on a steel structure without respiratory protection. The workers were wearing basic dust masks (nuisance dust masks) instead of the required NIOSH-approved supplied-air respirators or abrasive blasting respirators.

Air monitoring conducted at the time of observation showed airborne silica dust concentrations at 0.15 mg/m³, which is three times the OSHA permissible exposure limit (PEL) of 0.05 mg/m³ for respirable crystalline silica. The workers had been performing sandblasting for approximately 2 hours before the hygienist arrived.

## Injuries

No acute injuries. All four workers referred for baseline pulmonary function testing and medical surveillance enrollment per OSHA silica standard requirements. Results pending.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Workers used incorrect respiratory protection — nuisance dust masks provide no protection against crystalline silica exposure.
2. **Contributing Factor:** Proper abrasive blasting respirators were available on-site but stored in a locked equipment room. Workers did not have access to the key.
3. **Contributing Factor:** Supervisor authorized the work without verifying respiratory protection adequacy.
4. **Systemic Issue:** Respiratory protection program did not include job-specific respirator selection guidance for sandblasting operations.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1926.1153(c)(1) — Employer shall comply with Table 1 or assess worker exposure to respirable crystalline silica.
- 29 CFR 1910.134(d)(1)(iii) — Employer shall select respirators appropriate for the chemical state and physical form of the contaminant.
- 29 CFR 1910.134(d)(3)(iii)(A) — Assigned protection factors for different respirator types.

## Corrective Actions

1. All sandblasting operations halted until proper respiratory equipment is staged and accessible at work location.
2. Respiratory protection program updated with job-specific respirator selection matrix (completed 2024-09-10).
3. Abrasive blasting respirators relocated to staging area adjacent to sandblasting work zones — no locked storage.
4. All workers performing sandblasting enrolled in medical surveillance program per 29 CFR 1926.1153(h).
5. Supervisor received written warning for authorizing work without proper PPE verification.
6. Fit testing scheduled for all workers assigned to sandblasting duties.

## Follow-Up

Pulmonary function test results due by 2024-09-30. Respiratory protection program audit scheduled for 2024-10-01. Silica exposure monitoring to be repeated during next sandblasting operation.
