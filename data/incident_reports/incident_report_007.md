# Incident Report #007

**Date:** 2024-08-05
**Site:** Al-Obour Industrial Complex, Site A
**Severity:** High
**Category:** Fire Safety — Hot Work
**Shift:** Afternoon (14:00 - 22:00)
**Reported By:** Fire Watch Officer - Mahmoud Sayed

## Description

At approximately 17:40, a small fire ignited on the ground floor of Building 1 during welding operations on the mezzanine level above. Welding sparks and slag fell through open grating onto a stack of cardboard packaging material stored 4 meters below the work area. The fire was noticed approximately 3 minutes after ignition when smoke became visible to nearby workers.

The fire watch officer was on the mezzanine level monitoring the welder but did not have line of sight to the area below. No fire blanket or spark containment barrier had been placed over the open grating. A worker on the ground floor used a nearby fire extinguisher to suppress the fire before it spread. The fire damaged approximately 15 square meters of packaging material.

## Injuries

No injuries occurred. All workers evacuated the immediate area during suppression.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Combustible materials stored within the hot work exclusion zone (35-foot radius) below the welding area.
2. **Contributing Factor:** Open grating allowed sparks to fall to the level below without containment.
3. **Contributing Factor:** Fire watch was positioned to monitor only the primary work area, not the exposure area below.
4. **Systemic Issue:** Hot work permit process did not require a survey of areas above and below the work zone.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1910.252(a)(1) — Precautions for fire prevention during welding and cutting operations.
- 29 CFR 1910.252(a)(2)(ii) — Combustible materials shall be relocated at least 35 feet from the work area.
- 29 CFR 1910.252(a)(2)(iii)(B) — Fire watch required where combustible materials are closer than 35 feet.

## Corrective Actions

1. All hot work permits now require a 360-degree survey including areas above, below, and adjacent to the work zone.
2. Fire watch protocol updated — second fire watch required when work is performed over open grating or between levels.
3. Spark containment barriers (welding blankets) mandatory when working over open grating or near floor penetrations.
4. Combustible material storage relocated to designated fire-resistant storage area away from work zones.
5. Hot work permit checklist revised with photographs required of the cleared exclusion zone before permit issuance.

## Follow-Up

Revised hot work program reviewed with all welding crews by 2024-08-12. Fire drill conducted 2024-08-09. Insurance adjuster site visit completed 2024-08-10.
