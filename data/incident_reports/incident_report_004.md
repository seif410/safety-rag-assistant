# Incident Report #004

**Date:** 2024-06-03
**Site:** 10th of Ramadan Industrial Zone, Site B
**Severity:** Critical
**Category:** Struck-By — Forklift/Pedestrian
**Shift:** Morning (06:00 - 14:00)
**Reported By:** Warehouse Supervisor - Omar Farouk

## Description

At approximately 08:20, a forklift operator struck a pedestrian worker at a warehouse aisle intersection. The forklift was carrying a loaded pallet stacked to approximately 1.8 meters, which obstructed the operator's forward visibility. The pedestrian was crossing the intersection to access a parts storage rack and did not hear the approaching forklift due to ambient noise from nearby machinery.

The forklift made contact with the pedestrian's left leg at low speed (estimated 5 km/h). The pedestrian was knocked to the ground. Emergency response was initiated immediately.

## Injuries

- Contusion and minor fracture to left tibia.
- Worker transported to hospital via ambulance.
- 14 lost workdays. Worker placed on modified duty upon return.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Forklift operator traveling with elevated load obstructing forward line of sight — should have traveled in reverse.
2. **Contributing Factor:** No convex mirrors installed at warehouse aisle intersections.
3. **Contributing Factor:** Pedestrian walkways not clearly marked or physically separated from forklift traffic lanes.
4. **Systemic Issue:** No proximity alert or detection system installed on forklifts.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1910.178(n)(1) — Operator shall slow down and sound horn at cross aisles and other locations where vision is obstructed.
- 29 CFR 1910.178(o)(2) — If load obstructs forward view, operator shall travel with load trailing.
- 29 CFR 1910.176(a) — Storage areas shall be kept free from hazards, with safe clearances and passageways.

## Corrective Actions

1. All forklift operations suspended for 24-hour safety stand-down and operator retraining.
2. Convex mirrors installed at all 12 warehouse aisle intersections (completed 2024-06-07).
3. Pedestrian walkways painted and physically marked with floor barriers at all intersections.
4. Mandatory rule enforced: loads exceeding operator eye level must be transported in reverse.
5. Management approved procurement of forklift proximity alert systems (blue spot lights and audible alarms).
6. Forklift speed limit reduced to 5 km/h in all warehouse zones.

## Follow-Up

Injured worker returned to modified duty 2024-06-17. Proximity alert system installation expected by 2024-07-15. Monthly forklift safety audits initiated.
