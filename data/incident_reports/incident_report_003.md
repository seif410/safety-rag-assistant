# Incident Report #003

**Date:** 2024-05-10
**Site:** Al-Obour Industrial Complex, Site A
**Severity:** Medium
**Category:** PPE Violation — Head Protection
**Shift:** Morning (06:00 - 14:00)
**Reported By:** Safety Inspector - Khaled Youssef

## Description

During a routine site walk at 10:15, the safety inspector observed three workers in the active construction zone operating without hard hats. The workers were performing concrete finishing on a ground-level slab approximately 8 meters from an overhead crane operation. Steel beams and rigging hardware were being moved by crane directly above the area.

When questioned, the workers stated their hard hats were "in the break room" and that they had started the concrete pour quickly to avoid a batch timeout. None of the three workers had head protection within reach.

## Injuries

No injuries occurred. Work was stopped immediately until all workers donned hard hats.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Workers prioritized task urgency over PPE compliance.
2. **Contributing Factor:** PPE storage location (break room) was not adjacent to the work area, creating friction in compliance.
3. **Contributing Factor:** No PPE checkpoint at entry to active construction zones.
4. **Systemic Issue:** Absence of real-time PPE compliance monitoring in zones with overhead hazard exposure.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1926.100(a) — Employees working in areas where there is a possible danger of head injury from impact, falling or flying objects shall be protected by hard hats.

## Corrective Actions

1. PPE storage stations installed at all construction zone entry points (completed 2024-05-12).
2. Zone entry protocol updated — workers must verify PPE before entering any active zone.
3. All three workers received verbal warnings and documented safety counseling.
4. Overhead hazard signage updated with mandatory PPE pictograms at zone boundaries.
5. Weekly PPE spot-checks added to safety officer patrol schedule.

## Follow-Up

PPE compliance audit results to be reviewed at monthly safety committee meeting on 2024-06-05.
