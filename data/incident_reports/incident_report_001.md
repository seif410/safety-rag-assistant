# Incident Report #001

**Date:** 2024-03-15
**Site:** Al-Obour Industrial Complex, Site A
**Severity:** Critical
**Category:** Fall Protection
**Shift:** Morning (06:00 - 14:00)
**Reported By:** Site Safety Officer - Ahmed Hassan

## Description

At approximately 09:45, a steel erection worker was observed operating on scaffolding at a height of approximately 14 feet without wearing a personal fall arrest system. The worker was installing steel beams on the second level of a new warehouse structure. No guardrails were in place on the working platform, and the worker was not tied off to any anchorage point. A coworker on the ground noticed the situation and reported it to the site supervisor.

The worker stated he had removed his harness because it was restricting his movement while handling the beams. He had been working without fall protection for an estimated 25 minutes before being spotted.

## Injuries

No injuries occurred. The situation was identified and corrected before an incident took place.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Worker deliberately removed PPE due to perceived inconvenience during task execution.
2. **Contributing Factor:** Inadequate pre-shift safety briefing — fall protection requirements for the specific task were not reviewed.
3. **Contributing Factor:** Supervisor was not present on the upper level during the work activity.
4. **Systemic Issue:** No continuous monitoring system in place to detect PPE removal during active work.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1926.501(b)(1) — Fall protection required at heights of 6 feet or more in construction.
- 29 CFR 1926.502(d) — Personal fall arrest system requirements.

## Corrective Actions

1. Worker received formal written warning and mandatory fall protection retraining (completed 2024-03-18).
2. Supervisor assigned to maintain visual contact with elevated workers at all times.
3. Pre-shift safety briefings now include task-specific fall protection checklist.
4. Site management approved procurement of lighter-weight harness models to reduce worker discomfort complaints.
5. Investigation into automated PPE detection monitoring initiated.

## Follow-Up

Next safety audit scheduled for 2024-04-01. Worker cleared to return to elevated work after retraining completion.
