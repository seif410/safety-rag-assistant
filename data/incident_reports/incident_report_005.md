# Incident Report #005

**Date:** 2024-06-28
**Site:** Al-Obour Industrial Complex, Site A
**Severity:** Critical
**Category:** Confined Space Entry
**Shift:** Morning (06:00 - 14:00)
**Reported By:** Site Safety Officer - Ahmed Hassan

## Description

At approximately 11:00, two maintenance workers entered a chemical storage tank for internal inspection and cleaning. The tank had previously stored industrial solvents. Upon entry, the lead worker reported feeling dizzy and lightheaded approximately 4 minutes into the task. The second worker immediately assisted in evacuating the lead worker from the tank.

Investigation revealed that atmospheric testing had not been performed prior to entry. The confined space entry permit had been filled out but the atmospheric monitoring section was left blank and signed off by the supervisor without verification. Post-incident air monitoring detected residual solvent vapors at levels exceeding the permissible exposure limit (PEL).

## Injuries

- Lead worker experienced dizziness, nausea, and headache. Evaluated by on-site medic and transported to hospital for observation.
- Released same day with no lasting effects.
- 2 lost workdays.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Confined space entry performed without required atmospheric testing.
2. **Contributing Factor:** Entry permit was improperly authorized — supervisor signed without verifying atmospheric monitoring completion.
3. **Contributing Factor:** No trained attendant stationed at the tank opening during entry.
4. **Systemic Issue:** Permit-to-work process lacked independent verification step.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1910.146(c)(5)(ii)(C) — Atmospheric testing before entry into permit-required confined spaces.
- 29 CFR 1910.146(d)(5) — Attendant required outside the permit space during entry operations.
- 29 CFR 1910.146(d)(2) — Acceptable entry conditions must be verified before entry is authorized.

## Corrective Actions

1. All confined space work suspended pending retraining of all permit authorizers (completed 2024-07-05).
2. Permit-to-work process revised: atmospheric test results must be logged with timestamp and instrument serial number.
3. Independent verification step added — safety officer must countersign all confined space permits before entry.
4. Trained attendant requirement reinforced with dedicated confined space watch roster.
5. Four-gas monitors procured for all crews performing confined space work.
6. Supervisor who signed incomplete permit received written disciplinary action.

## Follow-Up

Affected worker returned to full duty 2024-06-30. Confined space program audit scheduled for 2024-07-20. All supervisors required to complete OSHA 7205 confined space awareness course by 2024-08-01.
