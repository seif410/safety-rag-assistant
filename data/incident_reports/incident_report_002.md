# Incident Report #002

**Date:** 2024-04-22
**Site:** 10th of Ramadan Industrial Zone, Site B
**Severity:** High
**Category:** Electrical Safety
**Shift:** Afternoon (14:00 - 22:00)
**Reported By:** Electrical Foreman - Mostafa Ibrahim

## Description

At approximately 16:30, an electrician received an electric shock while performing maintenance on a 480V motor control center (MCC). The worker was troubleshooting a faulty contactor and failed to verify that the circuit was de-energized before making contact with exposed terminals. The lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedure had not been performed. The worker was wearing standard leather gloves instead of the required Class 00 insulated gloves rated for electrical work.

The worker was thrown backward approximately 2 feet from the panel. Coworkers immediately disconnected power at the upstream breaker and administered first aid. The worker was conscious and responsive but reported numbness in his right hand and forearm.

## Injuries

- Electrical burn on right index and middle fingers (second degree).
- Temporary numbness in right forearm.
- Worker transported to hospital, treated, and released same day.
- 3 lost workdays.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Failure to follow Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures before beginning maintenance work.
2. **Contributing Factor:** Worker used incorrect PPE — standard leather gloves instead of rated insulated gloves.
3. **Contributing Factor:** No buddy system enforced for live electrical work.
4. **Systemic Issue:** LOTO compliance was not independently verified by a second person before work commenced.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1910.333(b) — Working on or near exposed de-energized parts.
- 29 CFR 1910.147 — The control of hazardous energy (lockout/tagout).
- 29 CFR 1910.335(a)(1)(i) — Employees working in areas with electrical hazards shall be provided with and use appropriate PPE.

## Corrective Actions

1. All electrical maintenance work suspended site-wide for 48 hours pending safety stand-down.
2. Mandatory LOTO retraining for all electrical personnel (completed 2024-04-26).
3. Two-person verification requirement implemented for all electrical maintenance tasks.
4. Insulated glove inspection and inventory audit completed — all workers issued new Class 00 gloves.
5. LOTO compliance checklist added to work permit process with mandatory supervisor sign-off.

## Follow-Up

Injured worker returned to full duty 2024-04-25. Electrical safety audit scheduled for 2024-05-15. LOTO compliance to be monitored weekly for 90 days.
