# Incident Report #008

**Date:** 2024-08-19
**Site:** 10th of Ramadan Industrial Zone, Site B
**Severity:** Medium
**Category:** PPE Violation — High Visibility
**Shift:** Night (22:00 - 06:00)
**Reported By:** Night Shift Supervisor - Amr Mansour

## Description

At approximately 01:30, a delivery truck driver reported a near-miss with a worker who was crossing the main vehicle access road on foot. The worker was not wearing a high-visibility vest and was dressed in dark-colored clothing. The road lighting in the area was functional but limited. The truck driver braked hard and stopped approximately 1.5 meters from the worker.

The worker was a maintenance technician who had been called in for an emergency pump repair. He had entered the site through the pedestrian gate and walked across the vehicle road to reach the pump house. His high-visibility vest was in his locker inside the maintenance building, which was on the opposite side of the road from the gate.

## Injuries

No injuries occurred. Near-miss only.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Worker entered active vehicle traffic area without required high-visibility PPE during nighttime operations.
2. **Contributing Factor:** Emergency call-in procedure did not include a PPE readiness check before site entry.
3. **Contributing Factor:** High-visibility vests not available at the pedestrian gate entry point.
4. **Systemic Issue:** No physical separation between pedestrian and vehicle routes at the main access road.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1926.651(d) — Employees exposed to vehicular traffic shall be provided with and required to wear high-visibility garments.

## Corrective Actions

1. High-visibility vest station installed at all pedestrian entry gates with loaner vests available 24/7.
2. Emergency call-in procedure updated to include PPE verification before personnel enter active site areas.
3. Reflective ground markings and pedestrian crossing signals installed at the main road crossing point.
4. Speed bumps installed on approach to pedestrian crossing areas.
5. All night-shift personnel required to have high-visibility vest on person at all times during shift.

## Follow-Up

Road crossing improvements completed 2024-09-01. Night operations safety audit scheduled for 2024-09-15.
