# Incident Report #010

**Date:** 2024-09-18
**Site:** 10th of Ramadan Industrial Zone, Site B
**Severity:** Critical
**Category:** Crane Operations — Dropped Load
**Shift:** Morning (06:00 - 14:00)
**Reported By:** Crane Operator - Hossam El-Din

## Description

At approximately 11:30, a mobile crane dropped a pre-cast concrete wall panel weighing approximately 2,400 kg during a lifting operation. The panel fell from a height of approximately 6 meters onto the prepared foundation area. The load fell because the rigging sling on the left side of the panel failed — post-incident inspection revealed the wire rope sling had visible broken wires and corrosion that should have been identified during pre-use inspection.

Three workers were in the drop zone at the time of the incident. The signal person had not established a clear exclusion zone, and no barricades were set up around the lift area. All three workers were able to move clear of the falling panel. The panel shattered on impact, sending concrete fragments up to 15 meters from the impact point.

## Injuries

No direct injuries from the falling panel. One worker sustained a minor laceration on his forearm from a concrete fragment. Treated on-site with first aid.

## Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Cause:** Use of defective rigging equipment — wire rope sling with visible broken wires and corrosion was not removed from service.
2. **Contributing Factor:** Pre-lift rigging inspection was not performed or was inadequate.
3. **Contributing Factor:** No exclusion zone established around the lift area — workers present in the drop zone.
4. **Contributing Factor:** Signal person did not enforce area clearance before lift commenced.
5. **Systemic Issue:** Rigging inspection and retirement criteria not clearly defined or enforced in site procedures.

## OSHA Standards Violated

- 29 CFR 1926.1404(a) — Wire rope inspection requirements before each shift.
- 29 CFR 1926.1404(c)(1) — Wire rope with broken wires shall be removed from service.
- 29 CFR 1926.1425(c)(1) — The employer must ensure no employee is in the fall zone when a load is being hoisted.
- 29 CFR 1926.1421 — Signals — required when the point of operation is not in full view of the operator.

## Corrective Actions

1. All crane operations suspended for 72-hour stand-down and complete rigging inventory inspection.
2. 14 defective slings identified and removed from service during inventory inspection.
3. Color-coded quarterly rigging inspection tag system implemented.
4. Mandatory pre-lift meeting checklist added — includes rigging inspection, exclusion zone setup, and signal person verification.
5. Rigid barricade system procured for all lift zones (completed 2024-10-01).
6. Signal person and rigger competency re-evaluation conducted for all certified personnel.

## Follow-Up

Crane operations resumed 2024-09-21 after rigging inventory and retraining completion. Quarterly rigging inspection program initiated. Near-miss recorded in site incident tracking system for trend analysis.
